Social Media Mining

Data Visualisation is an important aspect of Data Mining in order for researchers to better analyse their work, deduce non-obvious conclusions and thereafter be able to communicate their work with others in a comprehensible and accessible manner. To that end, in the context of a Web Mining project for the “Data and Web science” MSc program, our team has created this Web application analysing the social media discussion, specifically Instagram and Twitter, centered around the topic of Climate Change. The web page is separated into four sections, and we begin by presenting our collected data regarding Twitter’s most frequent Hashtags, Users and Words related to Climate Change.

Event Detection

In the second section, we present the plotted amount tweets and their 10 emotions on a day to day basis. This could be useful in identifying daily events, since an unusual burstiness in tweet count or a drastic and unusual change in a specific emotion could have been triggered by an important event related to climate change.

User Profiling

The truthfulness of human-caused Climate Change is practically undeniable, since the scientific consensus is around 97% regarding the issue, nevertheless, a significant portion of non-scientists, civilians and politicians alike, express doubts or outright denial against Global Warming. Our proposed model learns from the textual patterns of twitter users and tries to identify their opinions around the issue of climate change denialism.
